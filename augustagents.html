August Agents

- Responsive Web Redesign -

Reflecting efficient service through
a simple interface

Context

Based out of California and Nevada, August Agents is a “one-stop shop” for all electrical needs, streamlining project delivery with efficiency and expertise.

Role

I worked on the design alone with constant communication with the owner until I achieved their vision. Using HTML, CSS, JS and worked with the original web developer to hand off ownership and ensure a smooth launch.

Why Redesign?

The original site was mobile-optimized but not responsive to desktop or tablet views, missing key details and only displaying logos which could result in confusion.

Original Design

Client's Vision

Shooting for a consistent aesthetic with enhanced branding, the owner wanted me to match the style of their signature line card PDFs, with some unique requests that deviate from accessibility guidelines and UX Principles.

Objective

I aimed to transform the homepage into a hub providing all essential information at a glance, eliminating excess scrolling and disorganized content. Additional company details would be arranged intuitively, minimizing confusion. Overall, the branding and user accessibility would be significantly improved.

Analysis

Before making any changes, I wanted to take a deeper look at where the site could be improve.

Issues

  1. 1. An easily overlooked menu that contains only 2 pages, increasing the number of clicks the user must take.
  2. 2. Everything is blue and has a similar visual weight, causing all the elements to compete for attention; missing a clear hierarchy.
  3. 3. Unclear what this logo means or why it is beneath the CTA button.
  4. 4. An important button with low visual weight, same color as everything else, easily overlooked.
  5. 5. Spaced out with no details as to what these companies provide.

Final Design

Going off of the listed issues and unique design requests from the client, this is the final product.

Changes

  1. 1. Added banner to enhance branding
  2. 2. Easy to find dropdown menu of the linecards.
  3. 3. Black text to stand out from the blues and immediately capture focus
  4. 4. Two buttons that scroll for the user based on their needs. The detailed image behind, slightly clashes for attention, but this was requested.
  5. 5. Simple categorization allows quick glancing
  6. 6. Card format allows all info to be tightly organized and establishes a clear hierarchy
  7. 7. Reduced footer size while maintaining needed info

Conclusion

While I have no data or research to prove an increase in user satisfaction or conversions, the site owner is satisfied and I have successfully achieved their desired outcome.

Learnings

This being my first real project, and first ever deployment of a client’s site, it really threw me out of my comfort zone and pushed me to learn many new things quickly. Collaborating with a developer, using unfamiliar tools and gaining a deeper understanding of the development process was valuable as a UI Designer. It allows me to see the bigger picture and ensure a seamless hand off to developers.

What would I do differently?

Understanding and researching users is crucial. If tasked with this, I would interview them to delve deeper into their industry knowledge and what they seek from the site. This would allow us to optimize the experience, reduce time waste and ensure no barriers hinder prospects from making a call.

Future steps

Performing usability tests, conducting surveys and gathering feedback from users could benefit the design approach greatly. Until that is needed, I regularly maintain and make adjustments as requested from the owner as the company changes.
